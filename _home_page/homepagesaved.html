Introduction to the Digital Collections

The Libraries and Archives of the Museum of Fine Arts, Houston, are dedicated to preserving and sharing our rich collections of historical and cultural resources. Our Digital Collections highlight distinctive materials from our collections, which are freely accessible for educational and research purposes, enabling scholars and appreciators from across the globe to seek out knowledge and inspiration from our diverse holdings, including American friendship albums, Brazilian Constructivist catalogues, French weaving pattern books, Harlem Renaissance dust jacket designs, Houston artists’ sketchbooks, New Zealand fern albums, Soviet periodicals, and more.

Photobooks

Photography publications from the 19th through 21st centuries, including unique works, maquettes, and rare periodicals

American Art

Select American art publications from the colonial era to the Harlem Renaissance

Artists' Books

Distinctive artists’ books that exhibit a range of structural designs and printing processes dating from the 1960s to today

Decorative Arts, Design, and Craft

International selection of decorative arts, craft, and design materials produced in Austria, England, France, Italy, and the United States

Latin American Art

Latin American and LatinX printed matter ranging from Brazilian Constructivism to Mexican Modernism to Chicano graphics

Manuscripts and Unpublished Works

Singular works that span the 18th through 21st centuries, comprising friendship albums, scrapbooks, and artists’ sketchbooks

MFAH Documents

Foundational documents chronicling the Museum’s early history through manuscripts, annual reports, bulletins, and exhibition catalogues
