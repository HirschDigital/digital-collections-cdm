Introduction to the Digical Collections

The Libraries and Archives of the Museum of Fine Arts, Houston are dedicated to preserving and sharing our rich collections of historical and cultural resources. Through this Digital Collections site, we are now making many of these distinctive materials publicly accessible for educational and research purposes, enabling scholars and appreciators from across the globe to seek out knowledge and inspiration. The diverse array of materials and geographic coverage includes American friendship albums, Brazilian geometric constructivist catalogs, French weaving pattern books, Harlem Renaissance dust jacket designs, Houston artists’ sketchbooks, New Zealand fern albums, Soviet periodicals, and beyond.

We invite you to explore these collections by searching or browsing our holdings!

Photobooks

Photography publications from the 19th through 21st centuries, including unique works, maquettes, and rare periodicals.

American Art

Select American Art Publications from the Colonial Era to the Harlem Renaissance.

Artists' Books

Distinctive artists’ books that exhibit a range of structural designs and printing processes dating from the 1960s to today.

Decorative Arts, Design, and Craft

International selection of decorative arts, craft, and design materials produced in Austria, England, France, Italy, and the United States.

Latin American Art

Latin American and LatinX printed matter ranging from Brazilian constructivism to Mexican modernism to Chicano graphics.

Manuscripts and Unpublished Works

Singular works that span the 18th through 21st centuries, comprised of friendship albums, scrapbooks, and artists’ sketchbooks.

Museum of Fine Arts Houston Documents

Foundational documents chronicling the Museum’s early history through manuscripts, annual reports, bulletins, and exhibition catalogs.
