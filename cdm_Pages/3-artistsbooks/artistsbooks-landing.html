Artists' Books

About this Collection

Since the early 1960s, artists have employed the medium of the book as an artistic object in its own right, exploring and expanding the boundaries of the form. The Hirsch Library houses many types of artistsâ€™ books, including those that exhibit a range of structural designs, investigate relations between image and text, and display traditional as well as emergent printing processes. Particularly fertile areas of the collection are publications produced by artists Ed Ruscha, Bea Nettles, Dan Walsh, and Julia Borissova, as well as those published by Polvoh Press and those made by bookmakers in conjunction with the National Museum for Women in the Arts.
