Decorative Arts, Design, and Craft in the Libraries and Archives

About this Collection

With the establishment of the Latin American Art department in 2001, the Hirsch Library began to develop related collections that reflect the significant breadth and depth of the Latin American and LatinX Art holdings here at the museum. Our efforts to complement this area of growth have resulted in similarly rich research collections constituted by illustrated periodicals, biennial publications, manifestos, conceptual works, and poster productions from across Latin America and the diaspora. Noteworthy among our resources is printed matter ranging from Brazilian constructivism to Mexican modernism to Chicano graphics to Uruguayan universalism.
