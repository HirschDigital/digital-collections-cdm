Decorative Arts, Design and Craft in the Libraries and Archives

About this Collection

The Libraries’ wide-ranging decorative arts collections represent many countries, periods, and major design movements, including British and American works of the 18th and 19th centuries, the German Bauhaus, Austria’s Vienna Secession, and British Arts & Crafts. Among the highlights are cabinetmakers books and documents, including pattern books and price books, maker’s labels, invoices, and certificates. Trade catalogs provide detailed illustrations of works in silver and brass, and a unique sample book contains original samples of Morris & Co. wallpaper, identified with handwritten names and numbers.

Books and documents published by and for cabinetmakers, including design pattern books, price books, makers’ labels, and invoices.

Trade Catalogs

Manufacturers’ catalogs for the sale of objects, which enable the study of the history of design and production.
