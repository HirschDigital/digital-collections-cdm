Photobooks in the Libraries and Archives

About this Collection

From the Tate: "The photobook is a book of photographs by a photographer that has an overarching theme or follows a storyline - a convenient and reasonable cheap way of disseminating the work of a photographer to a mass audience."Early photobooks were used to illustrate the work of individual photographers or a new type of photographic process. Over the years, photobooks have helped to establish the idea that a sequence of images represents a narrative in its own right. Today photobooks are crucial for financing and cirulating modern phtogrpahy enabiling enthusiasts access to a wide range of photographers from across the world."
