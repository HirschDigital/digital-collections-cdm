About this Collection

From the Tate: "The photobook is a book of photographs by a photographer that has an overarching theme or follows a storyline - a convenient and reasonable cheap way of disseminating the work of a photographer to a mass audience."Early photobooks were used to illustrate the work of individual photographers or a new type of photographic process. Over the years, photobooks have helped to establish the idea that a sequence of images represents a narrative in its own right. Today photobooks are crucial for financing and cirulating modern phtogrpahy enabiling enthusiasts access to a wide range of photographers from across the world."

Over the years, the Hirsch Library has developed a robust collection of photography books. Photo book maquettes, dummies, and proofs are a specialized component of this collection and an area where we are making significant inroads. Maquettes, or dummies, are one-of-a-kind mockups created by the artists pre-publication, and proofs are single copy or limited production tests of books to be published. They represent the process of book creation. Digitizing these materials and presenting them alongside their final published versions offer invaluable insight into the shifting vision of the artist and the development of the book.
