American Art

About this Collection

American art and artists have long featured prominently in the Hirsch and Powell Libraries’ collections. Showcased within this section are newspapers, broadsides, journals, almanacs, government surveys, and memoirs that chronicle the country’s progression from its colonial-era ambitions to 19th-century expansionism to early 20th-century Modernism. These include masthead designs by Paul Revere, poetic expressions by Phyllis Wheatley, early lithographic views of the Alamo, and graphic designs by Aaron Douglas.
