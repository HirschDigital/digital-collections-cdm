Purpose and Mission

The libraries of the Museum of Fine Arts, Houston are dedicated to providing access to its special collections through imaging and metadata creation.  We are committed to supporting the scholarly needs of new audiences as well as our existing communities, including staff, scholars, artists, students, and the public as they pursue unique or distinctive elements of our research collections.
The libraries will prioritize materials that support museum research needs while secondarily supporting the needs of the broader art research community. We will oversee the selection, creation, delivery, and preservation of these digital collections, while welcoming input concerning the selection process to meet patron demands. As appropriate, the libraries will partner with other museum departments and external entities to support these efforts.
Materials considered for digitization are items held in the collections of the Hirsch and Powell Libraries. We will also provide access to born digital materials created by the MFAH and by local entities that are relevant to the museum’s research needs.

Copyright Takedown Policy

From MFAH Terms & Conditions, section 8:

DIGITAL MILLENNIUM COPYRIGHT ACT NOTIFICATION

The MFAH respects the intellectual property of others, and we ask Users of the MFAH Site to do the same. The MFAH may, in appropriate circumstances and at its discretion, take reasonable steps to disable and/or terminate access to the MFAH Site of Users who may be repeat infringers of copyright. If you believe that your work has been copied in a way that constitutes copyright infringement, please provide the following information to MFAH Copyright Agent:

A) a description of the copyrighted work that you claim has been infringed;
B) a description of where the material that you claim is infringing is located on the MFAH Site;
C) your address, telephone number, and email address;
D) a statement by you that you have a good-faith belief that the disputed use is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law; and
E) a statement by you, made under penalty of perjury, that the above information in your notice is accurate and that you are the copyright or intellectual property owner or authorized to act on the copyright or intellectual property owner’s behalf;
F) an electronic or physical signature of the person authorized to act on behalf of the owner of the copyright
